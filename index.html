Layer 1 · Thermal

Sulfate-Reducing Thermal Bacteria

Desulfovibrio thermophilus engineered for maximum exothermic sulfate metabolism. Creates 30–50°C underground thermal refugia from Mars's abundant CaSO₄ and MgSO₄. The biological space heater that makes every other layer possible.

Layer 2 · Soil

Acid-Producing Soil-Creating Bacteria

Acidithiobacillus and Pseudomonas producing citric, oxalic, and gluconic acids. Converts alkaline regolith pH 8–9 to plant-optimal 6.5–7.5. Releases 500–1,200 L of chemically bound water per metric ton processed. Forms 10–50 cm of true soil per year.

What it looks like: the pioneer era

Imagine the year is roughly 2055. You are standing on a path of reddish-orange compacted earth — not regolith anymore, soil now, with organic content and a pH the roots can use. On either side, domed structures rise from the ground. Their surfaces are rough and warm-toned, almost ceramic, built not from materials shipped from Earth but from the hardened secretions of cyanobacteria that have been converting the iron-oxide dust into calcified mineral composite for the past decade.

Above you the alders have spread their canopy. Between them, the fungal aerial webs — solar-powered climbing filaments the mycorrhizal network sent upward years ago — span tree to tree like a living pergola. The webs catch dust. They create microclimate zones of slightly warmer, slightly wetter air. Under the canopy, temperature swings are smaller than out on the open plain.

The pioneers on this path are still in their breathing gear. They will be for decades more. But they are outside, walking, under trees, beside a building made from biology. The biology came first. They are its guests.

Below their feet, two meters down, is liquid water — a thermal refugium maintained at 40°C year-round by the sulfate-reducing bacteria consuming the iron sulfates Mars has in abundance. At the edges of these pools, where the warm meets the cold Martian substrate, ice flowers form — crystalline structures of extraordinary delicacy, a physical record of the temperature boundary the bacteria are slowly pushing outward.

This is not the destination. The grandchildren of these pioneers, if the succession completes as modeled, will breathe. Their grandchildren will not remember the breathing gear.

FUNGAL KINGDOM

Mineral Maven Fungus

Rhizophagus irregularis engineered with 12 modifications. Mines minerals from the soil and delivers them to the tree's xylem system, which carries them up to the barnacle-slug slime glands on the branches — small sessile organisms that incorporate copper, silver, titanium, and quartz into a slowly-flowing glycoprotein slime that coats and beautifies every branch surface. The mycorrhizal network also connects Cathedral communities underground, carrying chemical signals and shared nutrients across the grove. The fungus is the internet of the community.

GASKET MOSS · DPC-GEN-MOSS-001

Living Seal — Leucobryum glaucum (engineered)

The gaps between guided trunks form organic lens shapes. Wild moss would overgrow the nacre panels. The Seal cultivar, engineered with four gene packages, grows freely in open air and stops permanently the moment it makes 2+ hours of sustained contact with any surface. The 2-hour threshold prevents false triggering from rain or insects. At contact faces, it lignifies — developing a hard weather-resistant outer skin from pine genes, while the interior cushion stays soft and compressible. In wet seasons it swells tighter. In drought it retreats slightly and recovers. It will seal the Cathedral for centuries without maintenance.

4 gene packages · thigmomorphogenesis arrest · contact-face lignification · seasonal hydraulic swelling

A robotic gantry positions electromagnetic coils around growing trees. ORGANISM 1 · LEFT-HAND HELIX

The Gardener

Spans kilometres to light-hours at maturity. Grows outward incorporating ambient carbon at ~1 km per million years. PAH-melanin filter lattice harvests 21 cm hydrogen spin-flip radiation and ionising radiation simultaneously. Iron-sulfur junction nodes amplify electron transfer. Over geological time — 10⁸ to 10⁹ years — selective adhesion creates structured chemical gradient zones: H-rich pockets, C-rich pockets, trace-metal concentrations. The Frolickers navigate by these gradients. The Gardener never intended to make a map. It just did.

left-hand chirality → anchoring · primary output: structured chemical landscape
ORGANISM 2 · RIGHT-HAND HELIX

The Cold Frolicker

Mobile. Joyful, in whatever sense that word applies at 2.7 K. Chiral helix antenna wound at 21 cm pitch converts electromagnetic radiation to rotational torque — it spins from harvested energy. Bilateral fins carry W₆-C₁₂ gravitational sensing dipoles for navigation. Calcium mineral shell grows thicker with age and serves as both armour and inter-Frolicker EM communication medium. One in ten thousand carry gold atom nanotube inclusions — these approach indefinite lifespan. When a Frolicker dies, its iron-sulfur clusters are scavenged by the living, carrying partial molecular memory forward.

right-hand chirality → mobile · heat bloom detectable at extreme range
ORGANISM 3 · PRE-ACTIVATION

The Dormant Spore

Contains the complete design of either a Gardener or a Frolicker — chirality set at formation, destiny encoded in the lipid maze folding state. Will not activate until three gates open simultaneously: H-bond site density confirms molecular cloud, gravitational dipole array confirms cloud structure rather than void, melanin harvest rate confirms survivable radiation. All three. Simultaneously. Can wait 10⁹ years. Has waited. Will wait longer if needed.

triple-threshold gate logic · 10⁹ year dormancy · destiny encoded at formation
ORGANISM 4 · MERGED FORM

The Merged Elder / Sower

When a Gardener and a Frolicker colony reach sufficient density and complexity, they merge. The resulting Sower inherits the Gardener's structural reach and the Frolicker's mobility. Its primary function becomes seeding: launching spore packages across the void between molecular clouds, carrying the complete design forward into regions where nothing yet lives. The Sower is the universe's postal service, staffed entirely by organisms that evolved to do exactly this without being asked.

merger of both lineages · primary function: interstellar spore seeding

Economics · Post-Automation

The Pillar Economy

Original economic framework developed since 2016 envisioning post-scarcity cooperative economics organized around 15 essential Pillars — food, shelter, energy, healthcare, education, transportation, communication, water, arts and culture, civic infrastructure, environmental stewardship, scientific research, manufacturing, social services, and democratic governance — each with its own democratic forum, rotational work structure, and resource coordination replacing market competition. The framework begins not with ideology but with neuroscience: the human brain requires the experience of contribution to function well. Any economic system that removes that experience — through automation, basic income alone, or enforced leisure — produces the neurochemical signature of meaninglessness. The Pillar Economy provides structure for meaningful contribution in a world where machines do most of the production.
